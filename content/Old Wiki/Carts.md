A Cart is a diesel powered machine that allows you to move on tracks and
complete the game. Carts are still functional even when destroyed, so
you can remove the wheels and put them elsewhere so long as you put fuel
in the tank. The tank can also be removed although it must stay intact
(not exploded) for it to work. The fuel tanks on the back of every cart
are very [explosive](Explosion "explosive"){.wikilink}.

There are 8 types of carts.

![Standard Cart](Standard_Cart_.png "Standard Cart")

## Standard Cart {#standard_cart}

The most common cart, it has three sets of wheels, with two of them
having stabilizers to keep the cart on the track. It sports four
buttons, two of which handle accelerating and decelerating, one which
starts and stops the cart, and one which honks the horn. It can set its
speed, start, and stop immediately. This is both good and bad, because
sometimes stopping or starting at high speed jerks the cart and causes
it to fall off the track. This Cart has a Fuel Capacity of **125**

## Test Cart (Engine Cart) {#test_cart_engine_cart}

The Test Cart outwardly appears similar to a normal Cart, but it has
more realistic mechanics than the standard one. It has a hood that can
come up to see an engine with four pistons, a filter, and a small car
battery. It notably has an acceleration time, rather than just going
straight to the speed it\'s set to, a delay before the engine starts,
and if you rev it too fast the pistons pop out and the cart gets much
worse. Overall, not a good pick over normal Carts.

This Cart has a Fuel Capacity of **135**

## Rusty Cart {#rusty_cart}

![Rusty Cart](Rusty_cart.png "Rusty Cart") The Rusty Cart is a worse
version of the normal Cart. Only 2 wheels work, the fuel runs out
faster, the start button along with all other controls are very loose
and will fall, and the hood will flip open abruptly at times, even when
not pressed. The cart will also fall apart when pressing the horn 10
times. Most Parts of the cart are loose as well, so it could possibly
fall apart any time while using it. If you have glue you can keep the
parts on but it won\'t make it so the start/stop button works again.
Generally a terrible choice even if you lost your cart, especially since
they use the test cart\'s mechanics and parts.

Despite this Cart being a worse version of the normal Cart, it still has
the same Fuel Capacity as a normal Cart. (**125**)

## Cartmaster Deluxe w/kitchen {#cartmaster_deluxe_wkitchen}

![Cartmaster Deluxe
image](Cartmaster_Deluxe.png "Cartmaster Deluxe image") The Cartmaster
Deluxe is found in the [Cart
Dealership](Cart_Dealership "Cart Dealership"){.wikilink} on the right
spawner. It functions similarly to a test (engine) cart.

The Cartmaster Deluxe is noticeably more bulky than a standard cart and
made of white marble. It has armrests on the left and right sides and
two seats instead of one inside. On the back there is a kitchen
attached. It has a hood which can flip up to reveal a stronger looking
engine compared to the test cart\'s engine, although the other parts in
there are the same. It\'s accelerate buttons increment by two instead of
5, and it\'s horn sound is a lot more dragged out and fancy.

Overall, this cart is not a good pick because it uses the test cart\'s
mechanics, but also because it loses fuel faster, accelerates slower,
and is heavier and bigger.

This Cart has a Fuel Capacity of **150**

## UltraCart EX SUPERGRIP TRACTION SINGLE-SEAT RaceCart {#ultracart_ex_supergrip_traction_single_seat_racecart}

![\"UltraCart EX SUPERGRIP TRACTION SINGLE-SEAT RaceCart\"
picture](Ultra_cart..png ""UltraCart EX SUPERGRIP TRACTION SINGLE-SEAT RaceCart" picture")
The Ultracart is, like the Cartmaster Deluxe, found in the [Cart
Dealership](Cart_Dealership "Cart Dealership"){.wikilink}. It spawns
from the left spawner.

The UltraCart is significantly larger than a normal cart, being much
wider and having an extra set of wheels on the other side to keep it
anchored to the tracks. It has two large holes in it\'s back for the
rocket engines, and has a much larger fuel tank than a standard cart.

It has the same movement mechanics of a normal cart, meaning that it can
accelerate and stop instantly, but it\'s accelerate and decelerate
buttons change it\'s speed in increments of 10 rather than 5. It also
has extra buttons, being the ENABLE ULTRA TRACTION which makes the
wheels have significantly higher friction, resulting in the cart
maintaining it\'s speed better, Oil wheels, which makes the wheels
slippery allowing the cart to run more smoothly. There is also a button
to reset the wheels.

As well as these, there is a gague that tells you **how fast it is
consuming fuel**, a modified horn that sounds like an old car horn, and
a final button that enables the rockets in it\'s back. It is NOT
recommended to use these rockets, because they are hard to turn off and
will usually fling the cart off of the track.

The benefits of the Ultracart are outweighed by one major downside; the
fuel consumption. It will run out very quickly while in use, resulting
in it stalling and stopping on the tracks. It is not recommended to use
this cart over a normal one.

This Cart has the same Fuel Capacity as a normal Cart. (**125**)

## Rocking Cart {#rocking_cart}

![Rocking Cart](Rocking_Cart_Side_View.png "Rocking Cart") The rocking
Cart is almost useless and can get you killed in many ways, do not use
it. It rarely spawns instead of a standard Cart.

It\'s main quirk is that the top is not connected to the bottom part and
instead has a gap in the middle. The cockpit of the cart is connected to
a bearing on the inside, and as a result will rock on turns, often
resulting in you falling off.

This Cart might have the same Fuel Capacity as a normal Cart.
(***125***)

## Console Cart {#console_cart}

The Console Cart uses chat commands, and is very rare to spawn instead
of a standard cart. If used properly this is the best cart variant, if
you can evade autocorrect since it\'s commands are punctuation
sensitive.

The commands are: ![Console Cart](Consolecartpicture.png "Console Cart")
computer, help - lists commands on the cart\'s console

computer, speed \[number\] - sets the cart\'s speed

computer, start the cart - Turns on movement

computer, stop the cart - Turns off movement

computer, honk the horn - Makes a terrible noise

computer, I\'m cold - Makes the cart fall apart and turn into 1000
degree parts, quickly killing anyone sitting in the cart

computer, I need to stretch my legs - Launches the seat out of the cart

This Cart has the same Fuel Capacity as a normal Cart. (**125**)

## 100K cart {#k_cart}

![100K cart](Carmelldansen_Cart_(100k).png "100K cart") The 100k cart is
a re-skinned cart with 100k pictures on the sides. It rarely spawns in
place of a standard cart. The cart\'s horn is replaced with a button
that spews confetti particles, makes the cart glow while changing color
every second, and also plays the entire song of Caramelldansen.

This cart functions the same way the normal cart does otherwise.
(**125**)

## Mini Cart {#mini_cart}

The mini cart is a smaller version of the standard cart. Rather than
resting on the rails like a normal one, it instead clings to a single
side. It has all of the controls of a normal cart and can be sat in.
![Mini Cart](Screenshot_2024-11-29_210738.png "Mini Cart") After the
initial parts, this cart can actually work, since it\'s only real
downside is that it can\'t handle intersections well. It is easier to
carry, easier to avoid traps, and overall a good pick if you carry it
across the track at the start. Its downside is it can\'t handle bumps as
well, so you should go slow on them.

This Cart has a Fuel Capacity of **90**.

## Portable Cart {#portable_cart}

The Portable Cart is another smaller version of the standard cart, It
can\'t be obtained like other Carts as the Cart can only be obtained
from the Portable Cart Gamepass. The Cart has a Fuel Capacity of **45**,
if you are looking for more info then go to this
[Page](Portable_Cart "Page"){.wikilink}.
