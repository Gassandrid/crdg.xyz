An actuator clock is a multi-part assembly in CRDG. It constantly
releases signal pulses to anything connected.

## Components

[Rubber slab](Rubber_slab "Rubber slab"){.wikilink} x1

Tripwire x1

Linear actuator x2

Bearing x2

[Cable](Cable "Cable"){.wikilink} (at least 1)

A base, preferably cardboard

## Assembling

Set down your base and then attach the tripwire to it, upright. Glue one
end normally and glue the second one indirectly with a rubber slab,
otherwise it won\'t work. Once this is done set up the actuators, also
upright one on the left and one on the right facing up toward the
tripwire, one extended and one not. After this glue the bearings to the
top. make sure they wont make it catch on the top of the tripwire.
Finally, activate the tripwire with the buttons on the side and the
clock will start. Use cables attached to the base to transfer the pulse
to other things so power cant come back and screw up the machine.

## Operation

The machine is controlled via the buttons tripwires come from. Press the
red button (disables tripwire) to stop the pulsar. Press the green
button to reactivate the tripwire and start the pulsar again.
