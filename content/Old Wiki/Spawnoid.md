![Beginning spawnoids trying to ride
carts](Spawnoids.jpg "Beginning spawnoids trying to ride carts") A
spawnoid is a nickname/umbrella term for a certain archetype of players
in CRDG. Most new players and people who generally are always at spawn
are considered spawnoids.

## General behaviour {#general_behaviour}

Spawnoids generally have newer accounts, do not chat, have no unlockable
items, and are mostly incompetent as to how the game works. They will
quickly find out basic knowledge such as how the grab system works and
how to operate carts, but cannot do so as effectively as a standard
player.

If they find contraptions they will generally press buttons without
asking or stay and watch to see what it does. Since they don\'t know how
the component system works, they will fall for traps involving tripwires
frequently.

If they have componement machines and glue, they will just spawn random
stuff and figure out what they do.

### Beginning spawnoids {#beginning_spawnoids}

Spawnoids who do not want to hurt people generally quickly turn into
normal players as they learn how the game works and talk with other,
more experienced players.

This process can be made faster, as long as they are moderately benign
spawnoids and use the chat. They will ask questions and learn more about
the game. If a spawnoid asks you a question, don\'t hesitate to answer
them honestly so that they can learn more.

Signs of transformation into normal players include but are not limited
to: asking before pressing buttons, talking in the chat, reliably
reaching areas past the cart dealership, building coherent things with
glue and the component machine, getting an unlockable item, and
generally being decent at the game.

### Hostile spawnoids {#hostile_spawnoids}

Sometimes, rather than becoming better players, spawnoids will become
more destructive as they learn. Regardless of what they are doing,
spawnoids will quickly find out that carts are explosive and try
throwing them around a few times. The more hostile variety of spawnoids
will specifically target players and builds, attempting to blow them up.
If their antics are stopped, they will either leave or start targeting
whoever stopped them.

Some spawnoids who are particularly younger users will grab things that
are from component machines and such and attempt to throw them off of
the map. If they find something attached to the ground, they will try to
throw it a few times before moving on to other objects. If it respawns,
they will still throw them off sometimes, although less frequently.

Hostile spawnoids are usually created because of the disposition of the
player, but sometimes it is because they were alienated by experienced
players being jerks to them and lying about game features.

Hostile spawnoids can make it very troublesome to escape the spawn if
they target you, and will throw carts to try to knock you off the island
or blow you up. They may also stack carts on the turn in the rails that
can be reached from the spawn.

## Defending from hostile spawnoids {#defending_from_hostile_spawnoids}

Generally the best strategy is to move to later areas. The closer to the
cathedral you get, the less spawnoids you will encounter and the safer
your things will be. If you want to build at earlier places, use spikes
to anchor your builds and consider using a flintlock to shoot carts
attempting to drive up to your island if you have it. Be aware that this
strategy may aggravate spawnoids, and make well-meaning players upset.
If spawnoids reach the island you are on, don\'t let them get to any
cart spawners, since they can use these to blow things up.

If you own the [makarov](makarov "makarov"){.wikilink} and the spawnoid
does not, shoot them repeatedly until they go away or die. You can set
up tripwire traps with guns taped to them to keep them away. If a
spawnoid is being shown to be particularly persistent watch and prepare
before they arrive so that they cannot do their destructive things.
Armed spawnoids are harder to deal with than normal ones but are still
relatively easy to kill.

If you have no flintlock lob
[jerrycans](Jerry_can "jerrycans"){.wikilink} at them once they get
close. be careful not to blow yourself up.

## Escaping the spawn when spawnoids are present {#escaping_the_spawn_when_spawnoids_are_present}

Spawnoids can make escaping the spawn very difficult, especially if they
are targeting you. Leaving the spawn will usually get you away from a
majority of most spawnoids, so it is important to know how to do it
under pressure.

If you are thinking of killing them, forget it. They will respawn right
there and continue their antics, so it is a waste of time since the
available weapon to the general public does too little damage. If you
think it will slow them down use a jerrycan to blow them up, be careful
not to get yourself too though. Grab a cart and set it on the track
quickly and leave before they go. If they chase you reach [Bill\'s
batteries](Bill's_Batteries "Bill's batteries"){.wikilink} as fast as
you can and prepare to defend yourself by throwing the rusty carts, or
setting one on the track and sending it to them.

If you own the [miracle of
momentum](miracle_of_momentum "miracle of momentum"){.wikilink}, stay on
the right side until the spawnoid comes near you then quickly lunge to
the other side, get your bearings, grab a cart, and go. If there are
multiple spawnoids on either side you will need to do it very quickly,
especially if they are coordinated and not fighting eachother.
