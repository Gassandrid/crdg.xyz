The transistor is a logic gate component in CRDG. It is a black box with
a blue input in the back, and a gray part on the front and front-sides.
It has a single LED light on the top, as well as dashes at the top-front
which indicate whether power is allowed through or not.

## Function

The transistor has two important parts, being the blue port in the back
and the gray part on the front. When powered through the blue port, the
transistor toggles whether the gray part is able to transmit
electricity. This can be used for toggles (turning buttons into levers),
and theoretically simple computers if scaled up enough. They are able to
replicate every other basic logic gate (AND, XOR, etc.) if multiple are
used and linked correctly, including (pseudo) RAND gates.
