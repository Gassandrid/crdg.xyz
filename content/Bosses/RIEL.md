## Description
**RIEL**, often referred to as **Riel** is a boss added on July 26th, 2022 and is often done as the second boss after [[Cart God]]. You have six lives inside of the boss fight, and losing all of them will kick you from the game for being "out of lives". Beating the boss awards the [[Flintlock]], [[Marbles]] (restricted to the [[Finality]]) badge), the [[Waypoint Teleporter]] (only in private servers), access to the [[QROT]] boss fight and the [[Ipecac]] sword inside of the Boss Hub. He has two attack patterns, which is less than [[QROT]] and [[Cart God]] but uses 9 attacks inside the attack patterns giving him more attacks than [[Cart God]].

## Attack Pattern
The attack pattern is always the same, and repeats indefinitely until the end of the fight:
- Teleport Slash,
- Red Laser-Beam,
- Ground Slam,
- Stomp,
- Teleport,
- Red Laser-Beam,
- Homing Orbs,
- Stomp,
- Ground Slam.

> [!example]- Teleport Slash
> As their first attack, they will teleport to you then slash, killing the player **instantly** if not dodged. This is indicated by a sparkle on the player's character.

> [!example]- Red Laser-Beam
> For their second attack, they will shoot a laser-beam, indicated by a red flash on his sword. The beam does continuous damage, so if not dodged correctly, can hit you for some damage or even kill you.

> [!example]- Ground Slam
> After the Red Laser-Beam, Riel will do a ground slam indicated by Riel facing your character. This ground slam also does continuous damage, so it will do damage if not dodged correctly. The ground slam reaches pretty far, so don't underestimate it at long range.

> [!example]- Homing Orbs
> After the second Red Laser-Beam, Riel will summon orbs above itself. After a short delay they will fly at the player(s). They do significant but not instant kill damage and are evenly divided among **all** players present.

> [!example]- Stomp
> This attack is always after the Ground Slam / Homing Orbs attack. Riel jumps up and then slams down into the ground quickly creating a shockwave that **instantly kills** any player that doesn't dodge it.

> [!tip] Fun? Fact!
> Riel temporarily pauses his attack cycle before using Teleport Slash to walk towards players who have moved out of his range. This usually applies when a player dies...

## Stats
Riel takes about **50** damage from each sword swing (although this can vary). Riel's health also multiplies based on how many players are in the server when the fight begins. For example, If there is only one player, Riel's health should be **50k**, if there are two players it should be **75k**, three players: **100k,** four players: **110k**, and five players: **120k**. If a player who defeated Riel enters, then Riel will have **+100k** hp(this doesn't get multiplied by the 4th and 5th player's health multiplier).

## Strategy
Much of the time spent fighting Riel will be dedicated solely to dodging its attacks. This is a long, drawn-out boss battle. Don't get greedy with your sword hits. Teleport and Stomp are instant-kill moves, while everything else is continuous damage. This means that you will take some damage if you don't dodge every other move perfectly.

Spend the first few iterations of your fight honing your dodging skills. If you can't dodge every single attack, that's fine - focus on dodging the insta-kill moves first, and tank some hits from the continuous-damage moves if you need to. Don't stand still during the fight: just moving out of range can eliminate the need to roll out of a Ground Slam, buying you time to get some hits in.

Try to hit more than one of Riel's body parts with one hit because each one does a tick of damage. You can do exactly two hits after the Homing Orbs attack, two hits after the Stomp, and two hits after the Ground Slam. This means you can (safely) land about 8 hits per attack cycle. 

Beating Riel is all about maintaining your rhythm. Anything that might break your focus can lead to death; so minimize distractions, and change your game settings. I set my camera sensitivity to be extremely high (2.6) to maximize the amount of times I could spin before lifting my mouse to readjust. Much of Riel's attacks are too quick to spontaneously react to - therefore, you need to predict the attacks before they show any signs. The best way to do this is by sticking to a specific rhythm, like `Q-Q-Q-Q-hit-hit-Q-Q-hit-hit-Q-Q-hit-hit-Q-hit` and repeat. Do whatever makes you comfortable. Don't wait for the opponent to make its move, or you'll be on the chopping block.

## Cheese Strategies
While it is *possible* to cheat, it is not often practical...

Using a well-timed macro to dodge for you seems to be an excellent strategy in theory. However, in practice, it becomes meaningless if the server lags behind even slightly: your perfect dodging streak, now stained by DEATH.

When carrying a friend, you can tell them to climb the arches of the door from the inside the boss room, and Riel wont be able to teleport to them. Rather, it will teleport *below* them in the doorway. This way, you don't have to worry about who's being targeted. Keep in mind, however... the carried player is still not immune to homing orb attacks: they will still need to dodge and pay attention to Riel's attack pattern in order to survive.
